# Renkang Guo, Ph.D.

## Translational Molecular Diagnostics | Digital Microfluidics | Data-Driven R&D Systems

I am a Ph.D.-trained biomedical engineer working at the interface of **molecular diagnostics, digital microfluidic platforms, nucleic acid testing, and data-driven R&D workflows**.

My work focuses on translating experimental and operational challenges in laboratory R&D into structured, traceable, and reproducible workflows. I combine wet-lab experience in nucleic acid extraction and amplification-based detection with practical digital system development for laboratory informatics, project coordination, and scientific documentation.

I am particularly interested in the **biology–technology–data interface**, where experimental platforms, structured data workflows, and translational R&D decision-making can be integrated to improve research efficiency, reproducibility, and product-oriented development.

---

## About Me

My background spans molecular diagnostics, sample preparation, digital microfluidics, and applied laboratory informatics.

I have experience in:

- Nucleic acid extraction workflows, including magnetic bead-based approaches
- qPCR assay development and optimization
- LAMP and CRISPR-based nucleic acid detection workflows
- Digital microfluidic sample-to-answer platform development
- Experimental troubleshooting and analytical evaluation
- Laboratory data organization, traceability, and reporting
- Cross-functional R&D coordination across reagent, instrument, software, and documentation tasks

In parallel with experimental platform development, I build lightweight digital tools to support R&D project tracking, sample data management, documentation workflows, and structured reporting. These systems are designed to make laboratory and product-oriented R&D processes easier to understand, easier to reproduce, and easier to act on.
